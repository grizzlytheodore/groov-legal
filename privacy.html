Privacy Policy

Last updated: December 18, 2025

This Privacy Policy explains how StudioTCHoldings LLC (“we”, “us”, “our”) collects, uses, and shares information when you use the Groov mobile application and related services (the “Service”).

1. What Groov Does

Groov helps you identify vinyl records (from photos), fetch album metadata (e.g., artist, tracklist, community stats), and find/play matching videos (e.g., full albums or tracks) using YouTube.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Account and Profile Information (if you sign in)

If you create an account or sign in (e.g., Google, Apple, phone), we may collect:

  • Identifiers: Firebase Auth UID
  • Contact / profile: email (if provided), display name, first/last name (if provided), profile photo URL (if provided), phone number (if provided)
  • Auth metadata: sign-in provider (e.g., Google/Apple/phone), account creation time, last login time
  • Policy acceptance: timestamp of latest Terms/Privacy acceptance (if this feature is enabled)

2.2 Scan History (per user)

To show your scan history inside the app, we store scan events under your user account. Each scan history entry may include:

  • Discogs release ID (reference to cached album data)
  • Timestamp of scan
  • Artist/title (denormalized for fast display)
  • Optional: thumbnail URL, year, and format

2.3 Cached Album & Video Match Data (shared cache)

Groov caches publicly available album metadata and video matches to reduce repeated API calls and improve performance. This cache may include:

  • Album metadata (Discogs): artist(s), title, year/country, labels/genres/styles/formats, catalog number, notes, community rating/have/want, pricing suggestions
  • Images: thumbnail / cover URLs and/or an optional cached thumbnail reference (e.g., a storage URL)
  • YouTube matches: a video ID and the search query used to find it (album-level; track-level only when you tap play on a track)
  • Cache timestamps: when cached data was last updated

2.4 Scan Metrics & Debugging Logs

Important: Groov keeps a scan_metrics log for analytics and debugging (e.g., performance, failure causes, cache hit rate).

This may include:

  • User ID (Firebase Auth UID)
  • Timestamp of scan
  • AI response time (ms), success/failure flags
  • Raw AI response JSON (e.g., the model’s output used to derive artist/title)
  • Discogs success/cached flags, failure reason, HTTP status, and error message when applicable
  • When available: resulting release ID, artist/title tied to the scan

2.5 Support Communications

If you contact us at support@groovvinyl.com, we receive the information you include (such as your email address, message content, and attachments).

2.6 Device & Usage Data

We may collect basic device and app usage information for reliability and security, such as device model, OS version, app version, crash logs, and technical logs.

3. Photos and Content You Upload

Groov lets you take or upload photos of records (e.g., cover, label, spine) so the app can identify the album and fetch metadata. Images you provide may be transmitted to our backend and to third-party services strictly to provide the Service (identification and metadata lookup).

We do not use your uploaded photos for advertising, and we do not sell your uploaded photos.

4. How We Use Information

  • Provide core app functionality (scan → identify → show metadata → play matching videos)
  • Maintain your scan history and account (if you use sign-in)
  • Cache album metadata and video matches to improve speed and reduce API calls
  • Monitor performance, diagnose failures, and improve reliability (including via scan metrics)
  • Prevent abuse and secure the Service
  • Respond to support requests
  • Comply with legal obligations and enforce our Terms

5. Third-Party Services

5.1 YouTube API Services

Groov uses YouTube Data API Services to search for and display/play music-related videos (e.g., albums and tracks). Groov may display YouTube video titles, thumbnails, channels, and enable playback.

By using Groov, you agree to be bound by:

  • YouTube Terms of Service
  • Google Privacy Policy

Groov does not use YouTube OAuth to access your personal YouTube account, and we do not collect or store your YouTube account credentials, subscriptions, playlists, or watch history.

5.2 Discogs (Metadata)

Groov fetches album information from third-party metadata providers such as Discogs. Cached metadata may be stored to reduce repeated requests.

5.3 Cloud Infrastructure (Firebase / Hosting)

Groov uses cloud services to host and operate parts of the Service (including authentication, database storage for caches and user data, and storage for thumbnails where applicable).

6. Sharing of Information

We may share information:

  • With service providers who operate the Service (hosting, databases, analytics, error monitoring)
  • With third-party APIs (e.g., YouTube / Discogs) to fulfill searches and metadata retrieval
  • When required by law, or to protect rights, safety, and security
  • As part of a business transfer (e.g., merger/acquisition), with appropriate safeguards

We do not sell your personal information.

7. Data Retention

We retain information for as long as necessary to provide the Service and for legitimate business purposes such as debugging, security, and compliance.

  • User account data: retained while your account is active (and for a reasonable period after for compliance/security)
  • Scan history: retained to show your history unless you delete your account (or until we delete it as part of account deletion)
  • Cache data (album metadata / video matches): retained to improve performance and reduce API calls; entries are periodically refreshed/overwritten
  • Scan metrics: retained for debugging and analytics; may include AI response JSON and error details

8. Security

We use reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards designed to protect information. However, no system is 100% secure.

9. Children’s Privacy

Groov is not directed to children under 13 (or the equivalent age in your jurisdiction). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

10. Your Rights and Choices

Depending on your location, you may have rights to access, correct, or delete certain personal information. To request help with these rights, contact us at support@groovvinyl.com.

11. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will update the “Last updated” date at the top when changes are made.
